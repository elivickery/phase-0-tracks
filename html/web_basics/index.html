Here are a few facts about puffins:
  • Puffins are "any of three small species of alcids (auks) in the bird genus Fratercula with a brightly coloured beak". (Thanks, Wikipedia.)
  • Baby puffins are called pufflings.
  • Puffins only have brightly colored beaks during the breeding season; afterwards, the colored part falls off, leaving behind a dull, regular beak.

Puffins are often hunted for their meat. Here are a couple facts about puffin hunting:

  1. Puffins are hunted by a technique called "sky fishing", which involves catching low-flying birds with a big net.
  2. Raw puffin hearts are considered a delicacy in Iceland.
